Hi, my name is Michael.

I'm a late entrant to the culture built by coders and hackers. For all my life, I'd only wanted to write and perform music. From age 6 to 26, that was all I knew. After highschool I moved from my rural hometown to enmesh myself in California's music industry. From 2010 until about 2017 I toured, produced music with notable artists, cohabited a art collective warehouse in SF, and overall had a really good time. The reality of that industry struck me hard in those later years, so I decided to explore other areas of life. I started studying part-time at a local community college. And now, in 2024, I've just graduated with a BS in Applied Mathematics of Computer Science, an experience leaving me captivated by computers, and the new mathematics we can discover to improve them.

I currently live in San Francisco, and thankfully I feel that I've finally settled on a culture and career that I'll cherish for the rest of my days. Lately, I enjoy spending my free time investigating different modalities of human-computer interaction. Whether it's working on novel audio synthesizers or training ML models for better cancer diagnostics, I'm excited about the potential to extend and expand human possibilities as far as possible.
